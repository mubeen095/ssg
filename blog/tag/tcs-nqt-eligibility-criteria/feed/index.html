TCS NQT Eligibility Criteria

The TCS NQT is an online test conducted by Tata Consultancy Services to hire fresh engineering graduates for various roles. Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) has announced the application and exam dates for taking the TCS National Qualifier Test NQT in 2024. This is an opportunity for fresh graduates to get hired by TCS and other major companies. This blog has listed the TCS NQT Eligibility Criteria 2024.

If you want to apply for the March NQT, candidates should submit their application before 5th April 2024. The actual TCS NQT exam will be conducted on 17th April 2024. The NQT is a general ability test that checks your verbal skills (reading and comprehension), reasoning ability (logic), and numerical ability (math skills). Your performance in this test determines if you get selected or not.

What are TCS NQT Eligibility Criteria 2024?

During the TCS NQT 2024 recruitment process, TCS with other big companies, will hire fresh graduate candidates based on their NQT scores. You are a recent graduate looking for a job at TCS or other top firms. You can apply for the TCS NQT exam 2024.

Additionally, candidates can also check out the application and Registration Process for the NQT Exam. Hiring Updates can also be accessed through this. The mock test is also available on our Skillvertex blog.

The age limit for the TCS Candidates is between 16- 26. The TCS Eligibility Criteria Table for freshers is given below.

The TCS NQT eligibility Criteria are such that candidates must have a minimum of 60% or 6.5 CGPA in their graduate degree (B.E/B.Tech) from a recognized university. Students in their final year are also eligible to apply. Candidates should not have any pending arrears/backlogs.

However, those pursuing integrated degrees like B.Tech + M.Tech must have secured 60% or 6.5 CGPA up to the 6th semester. The TCS NQT is open to candidates across all engineering branches like CSE, IT, ECE, EEE, etc. Having relevant programming skills is highly recommended to crack this online test.

What are the TCS NQT Eligibility Criteria for Freshers in 2024?

The TCS National Qualifier Test is open to fresh 2024 undergraduates (B.E/B.Tech) and postgraduates (M.E/M.Tech/M.Sc/MCA) from all engineering streams like Computer Science, Information Technology, and other branches. TCS NQT Eligibility Criteria 2024 for Freshers is listed below.

In TCS NQT Exam 2O24, The TCS NQT Eligibility Criteria for freshers are Computer Science. They should have completed 10th/12th, undergraduate, diploma, and postgraduate levels, you must have a minimum of 60% overall aggregate marks. You can have only 1 pending backlog/arrear while appearing for the TCS NQT exam.

Moreover, there should be a maximum gap of 2 years between passing your last qualification and appearing for the TCS exam. For example, if you completed graduation in 2022, you can appear in 2024 but not after that. The total gap in your educational career from 10th onwards should not exceed 2 years So small breaks here and there cannot total more than 2 years.

TCS NQT Eligibility Criteria for B.tech And M.tech Graduates

For B.E./B. In tech programs like Computer Science, IT, and other engineering branches, According to the NQT Eligibility Criteria, you must score at least 60% marks in 10th, 12th, and your graduation. Having a certification in programming languages is an added advantage.

Whereas, TCS NQT Eligibility Criteria, for M.E./M.Tech programs in circuit branches: a minimum of 60% marks in 10th, and 12th as well as your Bachelor’s degree (B.E./B.Tech). These B.tech or M.tech Graduates shouldn’t have any current backlog/arrears while applying for the TCS NQT Exam.

However, Consistent academic performance of 60%+ from 10th standard onwards is required for both undergraduate and postgraduate programs. Programming skills are preferred for B.E./B. Tech applicants, while circuit postgraduates must have cleared all their papers without any pending backlog.

Eligibility Criteria For TCS NQT – FAQs

Q1. What are the eligibility criteria for TCS experience?

Ans. Only full-time courses are considered eligible, not part-time or correspondence. Candidates must be at least 18 years old with a maximum of 3 years of work experience.

Q2.How much percentage is required for TCS NQT?

Ans. A minimum of 60% is required for the TCS NQT.

Q3.What is the TCS NQT passing score?

Ans. The TCS NQT cut-off percentage varies based on the number of candidates appearing. However, candidates should aim for an overall 60% score across all sections to clear the written exam stage.

Q4.Who is not eligible for TCS?

Ans. Candidates having an academic gap of more than 2 years are not eligible to apply for the TCS recruitment process. They will be disqualified if their total gap in education from 10th standard onwards exceeds 2 years

Q5.Does 12th marks matter in TCS?

Ans. For most Wipro and TCS jobs, you need to score at least 60% in your 10+2 (intermediate/higher secondary) exam. If you get 61% or more in 10+2 and clear the other rounds like the aptitude test and interview, you become eligible for the job role.

Q6.Is clearing TCS NQT easy?

Ans. The TCS NQT round tests candidates in various areas and has a high difficulty level overall. Aspirants should prepare thoroughly to crack this tough online test.

Q7.Is TCS NQT only for engineers?

Ans. Through NQT, TCS hires freshers with B.Tech/BE, M.Tech/ME, MCA or M.Sc/MS degrees from recognized universities. Candidates across all engineering specializations can apply.

Q8.Is coding mandatory for TCS NQT?

Ans.No, it’s not necessary to solve the coding question in the TCS NQT if you have done well in the aptitude and English sections by attempting most questions correctly.

Q9.Is TCS hiring stopped on Campus?

Ans. Yes, TCS has stopped hiring fresh graduates through the traditional on-campus recruitment process. The company now solely relies on the TCS National Qualifier Test (NQT) for hiring engineering graduates.

Q10.Who is eligible for TCS Ninja and Digital?

Ans. Candidates must have a minimum of 60% marks or 6 CGPA throughout academics. This includes 10th, 12th, graduation degree, and post-graduation degrees if pursued.

TCS NQT 2024

The TCS NQT 2024, known as the TCS National Qualifier Test (NQT) is an annual examination, conducted by Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) to discover new talent for positions like TCS Ninja and TCS Digital. The test comprises two sections, Part A, which assesses fundamental concepts, and Part B, designed for more advanced topics essential for roles such as TCS Digital. The next TCS NQT 2024 exam will be conducted on April and May 23rd. Candidates can also check out the mock test available on our blog.

Furthermore, Part B holds particular significance for roles requiring advanced technical proficiency. Excelling in both sections is necessary for securing positions within TCS, as they test differing levels of comprehension and expertise.

What is the TCS NQT Exam 2024?

The Initial TCS NQT Exam 2024 will be conducted on May 23rd and consists of 3 tiers, TCS Ninja and TCS Digital. Check out the table below.

TCS NQT Exam 2024 consists of two categories, the Foundation Section and the Advanced Section. Let us look into the further details given below:

a. Foundation Section:

The Foundation Section of the TCS NQT exam covers the fundamental concepts and basics of computer science and programming. It tests your understanding of topics including data structures, algorithms, programming languages, and problem-solving skills. This section will evaluate your foundational knowledge, which is essential for any software development role.

b. Advanced Section:

The Advanced Section of the TCS NQT exam has more complex and advanced topics within computer science. It includes questions, system design, database management, computer networks, operating systems, and software engineering principles. This section assesses your ability to apply your knowledge to solve intricate problems and handle more intricate programming challenges.

TCS NQT Foundation Section

This Foundation Section – the TCS NQT Exam 2024 consists of Verbal, Numerical, and Reasoning Ability.

a.Verbal Ability: This part tests your skills in understanding written English. You will read short passages and answer questions based on them. It also checks how well you can communicate your thoughts clearly in writing. Good vocabulary and grammar knowledge will help you perform better in this section.

b.Numerical Ability: In this section, you will solve math problems involving numbers, calculations, and data interpretation. You should know basic arithmetic operations like addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. Some questions may also require understanding charts, graphs, and tables to analyze numerical information.

c.Reasoning Ability: This section tests your logical thinking and problem-solving skills. You will be given puzzles, patterns, or situations where you should apply logic to find solutions. This section requires you to think critically, identify relationships between different elements, and draw conclusions based on the given information

What are the Objectives of the TCS NQT Qualifier Test?

The main objectives of the TCS NQT Qualifier Test are the following:

  1. Multi-Level Assessments – These will test the fresher’s skills and competencies. The TCS NQT 2024 exam has different levels or sections to evaluate their skills and abilities. It is designed to assess their knowledge, problem-solving abilities, and overall competencies required for entry-level roles in the company.
  2. Entry-Level Employment – This will analyze the competency in the cognitive tasks: The primary purpose of the TCS NQT exam is to assess candidates for entry-level positions within the company. It focuses, on evaluating their cognitive abilities, which refer to skills, reasoning, problem-solving, and critical thinking.
  3. Cognitive Skills – This will check the cognitive skills, necessary for entry-level jobs: Cognitive skills are mental abilities that enable individuals to process information, think critically, and solve problems effectively. The TCS NQT exam aims to test these cognitive skills, for entry-level jobs in the company.
