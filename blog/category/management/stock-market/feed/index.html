What is the stock market?

The stock market also known as the share market or equity market is a market where we can buy or sell a company’s shares.
Buying shares means buying some percentage of ownership of that company.
Selling shares means selling some percentage of ownership of your company.

What is trading?

Trading means buying something and then selling the same at an increased price so that profit can be made.

What is stock market trading?

Buying shares in the stock market and as soon as the price of shares increases selling it to earn profit is called stock market trading.

The Indian stock market has opened a lot of lucrative career opportunities for stock marketing enthusiasts as this market has been thriving for many years and is legitimately recognized for its strong and growing prospects. The jobs in this field are highly lucrative and give anyone a broad perspective and exposure to the World of the Stock Market. With the wide range of career choices available, it can surely be a hard decision to fixate on what to pursue. 

Here are some of the trending roles this field offers to enable aspirants to excel. 

Stockbroker :

Salary – average 3 LPA 

Role –  A stockbroker is the most essential source in a stock market. They enable the buyers and sellers to meet more coherently which places them in a highly crucial place in the cycle of the stock market. They are the connectors between traders and investors. This job requires a person well aware and updated on the market fluctuations, as the stocks come across different stages in the market, analyzing the patterns well, analyzing the patterns well gives an upper hand in the market standings. buys and sells the shares on behalf of an investor. A stockbroker is most resourceful when he/she rightly guides their client on when to buy and sell their stocks at the right time to incur more profits.

Financial or Investment Advisor :

Salary – average 4 LPA

Role –  Just like every other industry that functions stock market is also reliant on buyers, sellers, competition, and investment opportunities to survive in the long run. The market requires both traders and investors to be completely involved to understand the market and make decisions accordingly. This makes it crystal clear that consultations from a sound mind of market standings are pivotal. This is where Investment Advisors come in to ensure the buyers and traders invest and choose the right opportunities available in the market by creating strategies and eliminating financial risk.

Research Analyst : 

Salary –  average 4 LPA

Role –  Stock investors understand that their hard-earned money needs the right person who knows the market and is aware of analyzing and reading the market flow to decide on where their money gets invested in. Being a researcher or an investment analyst enables you to study and as well work on investment ideas. It’s through the evaluation you confirm if you can incur more profits or not. Daily work will include Financial Statement Analysis, Dividend valuation models, and valuation of common stocks using different ratios. Technically, a Research Analyst does in-depth research and interprets the client’s data, and recommends the stock. Research analysts use their expertise to study the market and develop curated portfolios and strategies to navigate at any given time. 

Investment Banker : 

Salary –  average 5 LPA

Role –  Raising capital for corporations, governments and other entities is the major role of an Investment Banker involved in the stock market. Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, and JP Morgan Chase are a few of the leading investment banker employers. The criteria and abilities these entities look out for are excellent number crunching, strong verbal and written communication, and the drive and capacity to untiring work long and grueling hours and reap really good paychecks. 

Relationship Manager :

Salary – average 6 LPA 

Role – Relationship manager helps firms improve their relationship with their existing clients and also acquire new clients. This position requires you to understand the existing well and build stronger relationships with new clients. They also have to be updated and require a great understanding of sales performance metrics.

Movies on Stock Market: Movies about the stock market have always been interesting to people because they show how the stock market works and how it affects our lives. These movies tell stories about the world of finance, where people try to make a lot of money, but sometimes they make bad choices because of greed. They also show us the risks involved and the consequences of those risks. Some movies are based on real events, while others create fictional stories. They take us into the exciting world of trading and show us how fortunes can be won or lost in just a short time. The characters in these movies are often ambitious and sometimes not very nice, and they have to deal with conflicts and challenges. We can learn a lot about how the stock market works by watching these movies, and they can also entertain us with their suspenseful and exciting stories. Some popular movies about the stock market include “Wall Street” and “The Big Short.” These movies continue to be loved by audiences because they teach us about finance while also telling interesting stories.

Movies on Stock Market Hollywood

7 Must Watch Movies on Stock Market Bollywood and Hollywood

Here’s a curated list of stock market movies that will help you gain insights into the world of finance.

SCAM 1992: THE HARSHAD MEHTA STORY (2020)

A series based on 1992’s biggest stock market scam committed by an Indian stock marketer named Harshad Mehta. It is adapted from journalist Sucheta Dalal and Debashish Basu’s 1992 book The Scam: Who Won, Who Lost, Who Got Away about many stockbrokers including him. This series does not glorify the stock marketer but teaches us about money and success in tangible terms. It also shows us various loopholes in the stock market world that we all need to be aware of before deciding to invest in the market.  

THE WOLF OF WALL STREET (2013)

This movie is based on the true story of Jordan Belfort- someone who pled guilty to fraud and crimes in connection with stock market manipulation and running a boiler room as part of a penny stock scam. This dark comedy shows us the loopholes in the financial market and how they can be easily exploited by one. 

It also portrays a clear picture of how greed can lead to a disastrous downfall in the stock market. It also shows how a man running behind millions of Dollars can easily break everything that helped him get there.

THE BIG SHORT (2015)

We are all aware of the financial crisis that took place in the year 2007-2008 in the US, where 7 million people lost their jobs. This movie depicts a dramatic tale of events that takes place during the financial crisis. It is considered to be one of the best stock market movies that not just focuses on debt securities but also teaches us about the trading concepts that all stock marketers should be aware of even in unprecedented times. Three individual but parallel stories are told initially about the US mortgage crisis of 2005. This gives a good perspective on how easily a market that looks really sustainable can crash suddenly.          

WALL STREET (1987)

This is one of the most compelling and popular stock market movies to watch. It revolves around a young and upcoming stockbroker who is willing to do anything to get on top including him tagging along with a ruthless, wealthy corporate raider. It focuses and enlightens us on different concepts related to the stock market that we might relate to in our daily life. The film shows how the market works and that it may crash down at any time. While it’s been three decades since the movie’s release, it’s still used as a tool to teach traders, marketers, bankers, etc., around the globe.

MARGIN CALL (2011)

This movie explores the zestful and fragile world of the stock market and how easily it can be disintegrated. It’s about an event that takes place in the early stages of the 2007-2008 financial crisis. The story revolves around a few crucial people at an investment bank over a 24-hour period and how they handle damage control in the market. The movie takes minimal effort in hiding how carelessly the banks function and explores the actual reason behind the financial crisis in the US. A great analysis of the current financial systems in our system.

BOILER ROOM (2000) 

This movie displays the importance of demand & supply chain in the market and how artificial demands can also be created to increase profits. The name boiler room refers to a large-scale operation used to lure in as many investors as possible to an investment scam using high-pressure sales tactics. It gives us a wider perspective of how greed clouds the thought process of investors and how they come up with schemes that would affect the whole system. This movie advises keeping emotions at bay while making effective decisions in the stock market world. 

ROGUE TRADER (1999) 

This movie is based on a book by Nick Leeson named A Rogue Trader: How I brought down Barings Bank and Shook the financial world, where he gives a great example of not going against the market. This movie also depicts the psychological functions of a trader who desperately hides his reckless mistakes. A must-watch stock market movie that teaches us about risk management and how financial oversights can be distressing and dreadful.
